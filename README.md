# Introduction to cybersecurity (Harvard CS50–based)

This repo is where I track fundamentals of cybersecurity and keep my own notes in one place. The main reference is Harvard’s free course **CS50’s Introduction to Cybersecurity**, aimed at both technical and non-technical audiences and covering everyday threats as well as the “what might show up next?” angle.

Official course page: [https://cs50.harvard.edu/cybersecurity/](https://cs50.harvard.edu/cybersecurity/)

That site lays out five weeks of material, assignments, and a final project. What you see here is material I compile alongside the course—this is not a mirror of the course, just a personal workspace that follows it.

## Why this course?

Cybersecurity is often sold either as pure panic (“everything can be hacked”) or the opposite shrug (“nothing will happen to me”). CS50 sits in the middle: risk versus reward, cost versus benefit, and the real trade-offs between security and usability. It touches accounts, data, systems, and software, explaining threats at a high level and drilling down when needed; I also like that privacy gets its own proper treatment.

For copyright and terms on the course materials themselves, see CS50’s license page: [https://cs50.harvard.edu/cybersecurity/license/](https://cs50.harvard.edu/cybersecurity/license/)
